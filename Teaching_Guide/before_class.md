# Before class

A ten-minute checklist. Nothing here is clever; all of it has gone wrong for
somebody.

## The evening before

- [ ] `make` — figures regenerate, any edited deck rebuilds, the index refreshes.
- [ ] `make check` — no deck should be flagged `review`. If one is, a slide
      overruns its frame and the bottom line may be cut off in the room.
- [ ] Open this session's [runsheet](./runsheets/) and pick your **cut list**
      now, not at minute 140.
- [ ] Decide which exercises run live. The decks carry more than fits; the
      runsheet names the two or three worth the room's time.

## The morning of

- [ ] Open the deck PDF **in a real PDF reader, full screen** — not a browser
      tab. Check the navigation bar at the top renders.
- [ ] Open the companion notebook and **run it once, top to bottom.** A stale
      kernel in front of sixty people is a long two minutes.
- [ ] Have the data loaded: the notebooks resolve `ISLP` → bundled CSVs → the
      book's site, but only the first two work without wifi.
- [ ] Second screen: notebook on one, slides on the other. If you only have
      one, decide in advance where you switch and put the cyan
      *Companion notebook* boxes on your cut list of moments to pause.
- [ ] Print or share the handout if you use one:
      `Teaching_Guide/handouts/chapter_NN_handout.pdf`.

## In the room

- [ ] Say at the start where the break falls. People settle when they know.
- [ ] The coloured boxes are a contract with the students — green is the thing
      to remember, red is the mistake everyone makes. Say so once, in
      Lecture 1, and they will read the decks correctly all semester.
- [ ] When an exercise goes up: give the time, say it out loud, and *stop
      talking*. The solution slide is always the next one, so you can move on
      cleanly when the time is up.

## If technology fails

- Every deck is self-contained in its PDF: no internet needed.
- Every notebook has a Colab badge — if a local environment breaks, students
  can run it in a browser with a Google account.
- The mock exams and their solutions are **not** in the repository. If you want
  them in the room, bring them yourself.

## After the session

- [ ] Note anything that ran long or fell flat directly in that session's
      runsheet — it is a plain Markdown file, and next year's you will be
      grateful.
